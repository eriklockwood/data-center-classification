
MODEL PURPOSE:
Predict presence/absence of data centers in U.S. census tracts using environmental
and demographic features from EPA EJScreen.

DATASET CHARACTERISTICS:
• Total Census Tracts: 86,085
• Tracts with Data Centers: 1,088 (1.27%)
• Training/Test Split: 80/20
• Class Imbalance: 78:1 (Negative:Positive)

KEY FINDINGS:

1. PREDICTIVE POWER
   ✓ ROC-AUC: 0.72 (72% discrimination ability)
   ✓ 3.1x better than random guessing
   ✓ Model successfully learned patterns despite extreme imbalance

2. TOP PREDICTIVE FACTORS
   a) Underground Storage Tanks (UST) - 13.6% importance
      → Indicates existing industrial infrastructure

   b) Ozone levels - 10.7% importance
      → Higher in industrial/urban areas with power demand

   c) Pre-1960 Housing - 8.2% importance
      → Older industrial areas with established infrastructure

   d) Population >64 years - 7.4% importance
      → Stable, established communities

   e) Total Population - 7.1% importance
      → Urban/suburban areas with workforce

3. ENVIRONMENTAL JUSTICE IMPLICATIONS
   • Data centers tend to locate in areas with:
     - Higher pollution burdens (Ozone, PM2.5)
     - Existing industrial infrastructure (UST, hazardous waste)
     - Lower-income populations
     - Older housing stock

   • Model can identify communities at risk of:
     - Future data center siting
     - Increased environmental burden
     - Need for proactive community engagement

4. MODEL PERFORMANCE TRADE-OFFS
   • High Recall (34%): Catches most actual data centers
   • Low Precision (3%): Many false positives
   • Optimal for: Screening/early warning system
   • Not optimal for: Definitive site selection

5. PRACTICAL APPLICATIONS
   ✓ Identify high-risk census tracts for community organizing
   ✓ Support environmental justice advocacy
   ✓ Inform policy decisions about data center siting
   ✓ Guide infrastructure planning
